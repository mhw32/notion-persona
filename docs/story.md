# Notion Personas Story

As companies grow, communication becomes the biggest bottleneck.

Not because people love meetings. Because decisions get more expensive.

A small product decision might affect engineering, sales, support, legal, design, and customers. If the wrong people are left out, the company can lose weeks or months fixing the mistake later.

So teams spend more time gathering input upfront. They ask for reviews. They schedule meetings. They wait for comments. They chase people down.

That process is slow, but skipping it is risky.

The problem is that every important person has more context than they have time to share. A sales lead knows which customers are blocked. An engineering lead knows where the system is fragile. A support lead knows what users complain about. A product leader knows which tradeoffs matter.

Most of that context lives in their past work: docs they wrote, comments they left, decisions they made, and principles they apply again and again.

Notion Personas gives each person a digital representative inside the workspace.

When someone writes a roadmap, product spec, launch plan, or strategy doc, they can tag the people or teams they need input from. Their personas review the document, leave comments, debate tradeoffs, and surface the context those people would normally bring.

The goal is not to replace human judgment.

The goal is to save human judgment for the moments where it matters most.

Your persona handles the obvious feedback, repeated concerns, known risks, and context you have already shared before. When something truly needs your attention, it can pull you in.

In a world where companies produce more documents than people can review, teams need a way for their knowledge to keep showing up, even when they are busy.

Notion Personas helps the right context reach the right decision before it is too late.
